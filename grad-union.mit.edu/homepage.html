Homepage

On April 6, 2022, Chancellor Melissa Nobles and Vice Chancellor Ian Waitz announced the results of the April 4-5 graduate student unionization election; congratulated current and past MIT GSU-UE members on their four years of dedicated work that culminated in the union’s election; and committed to bargain in good faith with the union over the terms and conditions of employment for bargaining unit members.

Those initial negotiations will get underway in September 2022 and are likely to take at least several months to complete. At other universities with graduate student unions, negotiating a first contract has taken at least one year (from the date of certification to ratification) and, in several cases, multiple years.

This website serves as a central resource with information about:

  • Which graduate students are represented by the MIT GSU-UE.
  • The collective bargaining process and topics that are considered mandatory subjects of bargaining and therefore must be discussed at the bargaining table.
  • Topics that are non-mandatory subjects of bargaining, which means that employers can decide whether to bargain over them or to work through existing channels such as Institute committees or working groups.
  • The latest updates, including letters and other announcements for the MIT community as well as, when finalized, the contract between MIT and the MIT GSU-UE.

Community members with questions about unions and unionization that are not addressed on this website may contact gradunionquestions@mit.edu.
